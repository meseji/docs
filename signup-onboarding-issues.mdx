<Warning>
  **Error encountered during Embedded Signup:**

  > "You cannot proceed with this operation since your WhatsApp Business account is currently restricted."

  This means Meta's backend eligibility systems have blocked your WhatsApp Business Account (WABA) from onboarding, API activation, migration, or Tech Provider linking.
</Warning>

## The "Verified But Restricted" Paradox

Many businesses assume that a clean dashboard means onboarding will work:

> _"My business is verified, my display name is approved, and my phone number is active — so onboarding should work."_

This is the most common point of confusion. Meta operates **multiple independent compliance, fraud, and integrity systems** internally. Your account can appear fully healthy on public-facing dashboards while being quietly blocked from backend API onboarding actions.

| System / Asset | Status |
| --- | --- |
| Meta Business Verification | ✅ Verified |
| WhatsApp Display Name | ✅ Approved |
| Phone Number Status | ✅ Active |
| WhatsApp Business Account Visible | ✅ Yes |
| Embedded Signup Eligibility | ❌ Restricted (Backend Block) |

<Note>
  This restriction is **not caused by** your website, frontend code, React implementation, or partner onboarding UI. It comes directly from Meta's backend eligibility database.
</Note>

## Why This Happens

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Existing BSP or Provider Connection">
    If the phone number was previously connected to another WhatsApp Business Solution Provider (BSP) — such as 360dialog, WATI, Interakt, AiSensy, Respond.io, Gupshup, or Twilio — the old connection may not have been fully removed.

    Meta's backend blocks new Tech Provider links if:

    - The old connection was never fully removed
    - A migration was left incomplete
    - Meta still detects an active third-party provider relationship
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="2. Coexistence Cooldowns & Eligibility Issues">
    Meta allows numbers to coexist on both the WhatsApp Business App and the Cloud API simultaneously. However, if the phone number:

    - Is brand new, or
    - Lacks sufficient active history on the WhatsApp Business App (Meta checks for a **minimum of 7 days** of genuine usage)

    ...Meta's integrity filters flag the Embedded Signup attempt as high-risk and restrict the action.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="3. Personal Admin Profile Restrictions">
    The restriction may not be tied to the business asset at all — **it could be the user**.

    If the personal Facebook Account of the admin completing Embedded Signup has any of the following, Meta will block the action:

    - Active account flags or identity verification issues
    - Active advertising bans or "Meta Technologies" management restrictions
    - Any history of policy enforcement on the personal account
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="4. Past Policy or Template Violations">
    Even if the account is currently marked "Active," historical negative markers leave a **risk trail** on Meta's backend:

    - Past spikes in user spam reports
    - Message templates repeatedly rejected by automated moderation
    - Drops in phone number quality ratings
    - Minor commerce policy violations

    Old violations can affect future onboarding eligibility.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="5. Unofficial API or Automation Software Usage">
    If the phone number was ever used with:

    - Unofficial, non-Meta web automation tools
    - Modified WhatsApp APKs
    - Desktop bulk-sending software
    - Scraper scripts or tools violating Meta policy

    Meta permanently logs that number's footprint in their backend risk engine. **This restriction rarely appears on standard account summary screens.**
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="6. Meta Integrity & Trust Restrictions (Silent Flags)">
    Meta uses automated risk-scoring algorithms that can silently restrict:

    - API onboarding
    - Partner linking
    - Tech Provider access
    - Cloud API activation

    Triggers can include sudden IP changes during setup, mismatched billing regions, or registering multiple WABAs in a short timeframe. These are **completely separate from standard business verification**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Step-by-Step Diagnostics

Before submitting any appeals, audit these three areas to locate where the block lives.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Meta Account Quality">
    Go to the [Meta Account Quality Dashboard](https://business.facebook.com/accountquality).

    Look for:

    - Restricted or disabled WhatsApp assets
    - Integrity violations or policy warnings
    - Personal account limitations on the admin profile
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open WhatsApp Manager">
    Navigate to your [WhatsApp Manager](https://business.facebook.com/wa/manage/home).

    Check:

    - Phone number status and quality rating
    - Currently connected provider
    - Pending migration issues or onboarding warnings
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Meta Business Settings">
    Open [Meta Business Settings](https://business.facebook.com/settings) → **Accounts → WhatsApp Accounts**.

    Verify:

    - The WABA belongs to the correct Business Portfolio (formerly Business Manager)
    - No conflicting third-party partner/provider is still connected
    - No ownership conflicts or duplicate WABA entries exist
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to Fix This

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Option 1 — Request Meta Review">
    Open [Meta Business Support](https://www.facebook.com/business/help) and submit a ticket for **WhatsApp Business API Onboarding Eligibility**.

    Use this exact message to bypass front-line bot responses:

    ```text
    Embedded Signup is failing with the error: "WhatsApp Business account is currently restricted"
    (Error Code #2655121). The Meta Business Portfolio is verified and healthy, but internal
    API onboarding and partner-linking is blocked. Please trigger a manual engineering review
    of the WABA's backend integrity status.
    ```

    <Note>
      Referencing **Error Code #2655121** explicitly in your ticket significantly improves routing to the correct support team.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Option 2 — Remove Old BSP Connections">
    If the number was previously connected to another provider:

    1. Log into the previous provider's platform
    2. Explicitly **disconnect or delete** the WhatsApp integration
    3. Ensure the migration process is fully finalized
    4. Remove any unused third-party integrations
    5. **Wait 15–30 minutes** for Meta's cache to sync
    6. Retry Embedded Signup
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Option 3 — Manual Partner Mapping">
    If Embedded Signup keeps failing but the WABA is visible in the client's Meta Business Portfolio, try a manual handshake:

    1. Go to **Business Settings → Accounts → WhatsApp Accounts**
    2. Select the target WABA
    3. Click **Assign Partners**
    4. Enter your Tech Provider's Meta Business ID
    5. Grant **Manage WhatsApp Account** permissions manually
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Option 4 — Use a Fresh Phone Number">
    If Meta's risk engine has permanently flagged the number, the appeals process can take weeks.

    The fastest resolution is often:

    - Provision a **completely clean, unused phone number**
    - Create a **fresh WABA** on that number
    - Onboard again through Embedded Signup

    <Warning>
      Do not reuse the same number with a different SIM or carrier. The restriction is tied to the number itself, not the physical device.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Partner Access: Important Note

<Warning>
  Adding a Tech Provider as a partner **does NOT automatically grant WhatsApp access**.
</Warning>

Customers must manually complete these steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open WhatsApp Accounts">
    In Meta Business Settings, navigate to **Accounts → WhatsApp Accounts**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the WABA">
    Click on the specific WhatsApp Business Account you want to link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign Partner Access">
    Click **Assign Partners**, enter the Tech Provider's Business ID, and enable **WhatsApp management permissions**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Without this manual step, WhatsApp assets will not display correctly during onboarding.
